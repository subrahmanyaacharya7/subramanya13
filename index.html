About the Temple

The temple is renowned for its significance in the worship of Lord Subramanya, who is believed to be the protector from all kinds of poisonous bites and diseases.Significance: Religious Importance: The temple is a major pilgrimage site, particularly for devotees seeking relief from serpentine afflictions (Sarpa Dosha) and other problems. It is believed that worshipping Lord Subramanya here can bring blessings and alleviate various troubles. Historical and Mythological Connections: The temple is associated with several legends and historical events. It is believed to be the place where Lord Subramanya, the son of Lord Shiva, killed the demon Tharaka, who was a threat to the gods. Festival Celebrations: The temple is a center for various religious festivals, including the annual "Karthika Deepam" festival, which attracts a large number of devotees. Other significant festivals include "Subramanya Shasti" and "Sankashti Chaturthi."
